A brief history of the one of the Oldest groups in the Belgian Scene
In the beginning there were 3 young students (at that moment 5th year (1991-1992) of mathematics) with a large interrests towards Computers with a stress on Computer Graphics, those three guys were : Sleeping Dog(at that time called Bitfreak), Airborne and Black Hellcat Jr. (at that time Merlin Mustang).

Back in those days Black Hellcat Jr. wrote the first intro released by our group, the Einstein Intro. It was an intro with a picture of Albert, the info about the BBS and some new effects which were seen in many other demos afterwards... (ever seen a figure walking on the screen ?? (Some Assembly '93 demo), bouncing balls that get smaller when they reach the floor ?? (don't remember where I saw this again..), Moving the screenrows in a sin wave ? It were the early tries of a coder that had great capacities but not enough time. (The Information on the picture is incorrect !!! )

A groupname had to be found... hmm... we kind of hated the hacking crew called Future Brain Inc. so we decided to call our group CIA, spending some hours to find a propper abbreviation for it was no problem at all.. we were coders that were in Action.. the group was founded Coders In Action.

Airborne was working on his Gras (GRaphics Animation System), which he never finished, Black Hellcat was working on some other routines and Sleeping Dog was awake, looking for some other members, and also coding the last thing he coded till this day. The EgaCia intro. A bbs intro for that same Einstein BBS but then in EGA Because back in those days he was the proud owner of an EGA screen.

Then sudden he met Purple Zebra, who had released The Temple of Love (Silicon Mage) after a couple of months Purple Zebra joined CIA At that moment we also had our music Artist, Jan, but he was so inactive that we didn't use any of his modules. Well I'm not going to type a list of what we released so far because you can find that in the end of this page.

We found a graphician, Fatal Black who drew a couple of nice pictures that were e.g. used in Simm Zone. Well... we started 1994 with some good ideas. We had been active in the scene for more than one and a half year now.. so we decided to go to some parties. The first were we could go to was Bizarre'94, (Nijmegen) it was not that difficult to reach and we had a nice production finished.

So off we were... Purple Zebra and Sleeping dog at their first demo party. We met lot's of interresting people there. (not going to greet them). But I'm still looking for somebody who can tell me the not faked results. On other parties lot's of people told me that the organisation changed some results, and another group should have won, so we still don't know how high Simm Zone was ranked, but it was High !

We had cought the partyvirus, nothing could stop us to go to Assembly '94,(Helsinki) us were Purple Zebra, Emperor (who actually became an official member during Assembly) and Sleeping Dog. There we felt like changing the group name to The Natives, 'cause there were too much Cia's on this world.

In the meanwhile we were contacted by Mhoram, one of the best music artists in the world, and he joined us, the Internet rules... fast communication between some guys in Belgium and our USA Music-artist ! (Did i mention he came 1Oth on Music Contest ][ ?

So we did.. Next party coming up... Wired'94. We simply HAD to go so the four of us did. Purple Zebra, Emperor, AirMax ,and Sleeping Dog Airmax was our newest Graphician and he's cool...

Around this time Sleeping Dog also met Uncle bob from Zion, a brand new group where the main coder only lived 5 minutes from Sleeping Dog.

So that was it for the biggest part of the group, they had to study. But there was one who wanted to go to them al... he would and should go to the last one : The Party IV. And he did... met lot's of people and had more fun than ever before. After 2.5 years the Natives are now on Top Speed, It's almost impossible that you won't hear something from us you will.



Zion was to small to survive and died. The Members of Zion that wanted to go on with the Scene joined the Natives. Thus Uncle Bob (Coder) And Balex-T (Gfx) joined The Natives.

Time to go to some other party then. The Meeting '95 Lille / France Sleeping Dog and Uncle Bob going to there. With only one goal : meeting some people. But early during the first moring a second goal ecame fact. We had to win the 4k intro. After 3.5 hours of work our intro was finished. And guess what. We won the compo !

Half april 1994 we attended a brand new party in Holland... X'95, great atmosphere, perfect organising and really cool releases... check out hagar for the releases ! Congratulations to the SuccesS team, and we'll be back at X'96 if nothing serious happens !

Because Synergy Design went completely to Game Coding Tom Verbeure decided to join us and do some additional coding. We gladly welcome him ! Mhoram's brother , Crow, was also interrested in doing some music for us, which we also found very interresting.

Then a bunch of exams came. No one of us had the money to go to Juhla, Icing, Bush, and other great parties held in Finland.

Luckily there was Somewhere in Holland one week week after the exams. Although the party organisation was rather bad we had a good time, we were Purple Zebra, Emperor, Airmax, Balex-T, Uncle Bob, Tom and Sleeping Dog. We actually planned a party report from Sih'95 but since we can't find any 'videoblaster' at the moment we haven't been able to digitise the pictures yet :(

Then Uncle Bob and Sleeping Dog went to Assembly'95 together with some Pure and Tfl/TdV dudes. 3 hours before the deadline Uncle Bob finally started his 4kb intro which became only 2.7kb with 7 parts, this was obviously not enough to enter the top 15, because we didn't have any flat phong shading with 6500 polys.. (that were the 1.4 kb we had left but we had no time to get rid of those 100 bits... we should have removed all the metal shading and we didn't think it was worth showing the crowd that we had metal shading in a 4kb while nobody else had ever showed metal shadin in a demo yet).

Time for Bizarre'95 then, this time only 35 mins of driving, Sleeping Dog had to go alone due to the lousiness of the other members. Bizarre was fun, meeting friends, and projecting Wired Advertisments on the Big Screen. Afterall Bizarre'95 was a lot better than Bizarre'94, probably we'll got to Bizarre'96 also. There's one picture from Bizarre'95 I simply have to show you. It's the winning picture from Contrast/Acme. It's too good not to show you

Wired'95 was one of the best parties ever.. certainly a top 3 party ever... Well.. we fucked up on our demo.. forget to vote and stuff like that.. but still we made what some people call the best belgian demo ever.. at this moment we are still waiting for the final bugfix from Uncle Bob.. but it should be out any moment right now..

At the moment the only party we are looking forward to is The Party'95 it's not 100% sure but probably we'll be there..
