What is Fuel?

Fuel is a group of PC-loving people, who have a common interest: creating computer graphics, music and demonstrations.

History

Fuel was born in late December 1994, and was founded by three guys, named Ben Hurry, Mailman and Brainbreaker. At the time (and at this very moment again =) they were doing 1st year of Informatics at the university in Belgium. So the origin of Fuel lies in Belgium.
Early 1995, Fuel got some new members, including me (Deep Freezer) and The Knight, and step by step, the both of us took over the organising in Fuel.

Back in those days, we released loads of stuff, like stand-alone modules, FLI's etc. That's why we'd rather forget about those days =)

Since the summer of '95, we were able to increase our quality level by kicking all the lamer members, and being much more selective in accepting new members. During that summer, we released two music packs, we showed up at two parties in Holland, being Bizarre '95 and Somewhere In Holland '95, where we competed with music and ansi's, but the final breakthrough was at the Wired'95, the party of our first demo (which ended as second Belgian demo, right after TFL/TDV) and our winning and 4th place ansi's, together with some music, kick-ass T-shirts =) , new members, foreign contacts etc...
In the mean time, we were also present at the Belgian Scene Barbecue (by The Natives) in September '95, and mid December '95 we were having 'a couple' of beers at the Belgian Scene Meeting in caf‚ The Blokhut, Leuven/Belgium.

Releases

  • Demo's:
    So far, we've released only one demo, which we presented at the Wired '95 party, held in Mons/Belgium. The demo is called Naft, and was finished in only a week, but we're satisfied about it, and it looks like more people are, because after the party, we got some new members, including Kovax! (musician / ex-Valhalla), Black Lion (musician / ex-Imphobia), Ghory (gfx / ex-Isch Crew) and Mabuse (coder / ex-Isch Crew).
    The demo contains some nice gfx, a couple of Fuel(c) bitmap effects =) , a warp of Sleeping Dog's face :) , a gouraud shaded 3D bitmapped kubus with fire-effect, a sphere with sunglass-mapping (really! :), and some cool Lenny Kravitz-alike music.

  • Ansi's:
    Fuel has a very strong ansi sections. Maybe one of the best of Europe?
    At this moment, our latest pack number 7 has been released. It contains quality artwork from all our ansi members. We're also busy coding an ansi viewer with VGA and SVGA viewmodes. As soon as it'll be finished, we will include it in all our future ansi packs. And since The Knight engaged on the Internet, we got some cool foreign ansi drawers as well.
    At the Wired party, Thrasher got the first place with his contribution, The Knight ended fourth, and we're sure that Necrofiliac would also have had a nice place if the organisers didn't forget to list him in the final results. (He wasn't disqualified!)

  • Fusion:
    This is the bame of the Belgian Scene Magazine, of which the zero-issue has been released just before Wired '95, and the next issue should be out real soon. First, it was coded by Rex Nebular, and it ran under DOS, OS/2, Windows and Win95, in 256 color mode, with some nice gfx, and some nice tunes. But for personal reasons, Rex Nebular had to quit the scene some time, and at this moment, Access/Antares is coding the new magazine-engine, also in VESA 256 color mode, this time also with scrolling.
    Some moe things about the contents: Fusion is purely BY and FOR the Belgian scene. Generally, it has the same contents as any other magazine, but only Belgian-related matters are included in Fusion.
    Instead of talking so much, you'd maybe better take a look at the latest release, or at the Fusion homepage.
    If you are interested in writing for Fusion, then take a look at the bottom of this page on how to contact Deep Freezer.

    Belgian Scene Meeting

    In Holland, there is a 2-monthly based gathering of scene people, called the SuccesS meetings (which are organised by SucceSS - difficult, huh?) Sleeping Dog got the idea of doing much the same here in Belgium, and the first Belgian Scene Meeting took already place. It was in a caf‚ in the ultimate student city in Belgium, called Leuven. There were about 20 people from different groups whole over Belgium, including Darkness/Imphobia, Sleeping Dog/The Natives, some TFL/TDV members, Pointer/Digital House, some Alpha 574 guys, and base Fuel crew.

    In the future, other BSM's might get organised at the same place, or in another city. If you feel like having a beer with the rest of the Belgian Scene, then watch out for upcoming invitations!

    The Future

    In the future, we'll continue releasing quality ansipacks, demo's, whatever... We constantly try to iuncrease our quality-level, and won't release something until it meets those needs.
    As long as there is enough interest, and enough writers, we'll continue to release the Fusion magazine.
    And ofcourse we will also be present at future parties, for now being Mekka'96, Somewhere In Holland'96, Bizarre'96 and Wired'96. And if there might follow other Belgian Scene Meeting's or Barbecues, we sure to catch us over there!
